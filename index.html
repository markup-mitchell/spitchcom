About

Maybe if I had grown up with the internet, web development would have been an obvious choice.

I was obsessed with computers, and art, and spent hours grafting together scraps of leftover Lettraset into various Frankenstein typefaces...

But it was was not to be, and gradually I lost any aspiration to work in a creative industry. My analytical temprement saw me through a degree in philosophy and into a career in customer service, technical support, operations and, latterly, project management.

This has been a very worthwhile process in terms of learning about organistions, business and people, but I've reached a point where I have to find real meaning and enjoyment in my work.

Over the past few years I have rekindled my love of computing and, for the first time in my life, have a true sense of vocation.
