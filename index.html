About

I used to hate computers so much. All through my 20s I battled with desktop and laptop machines. Scanning, word processing, working with photographs and music. Everything was difficult and counterintuitive. Things happened that I didn't want to happen. Things wouldn't happen when I did want them to. Whenever I used my computer I felt frustrated, enraged.

But what could I do? I was too old to learn how to deal with modern computers. The days of my ZX Spectrum 48K+ had gone, and I had failed to keep up. I was resigned to feeling put-upon... powerless... oppressed by this technology that should have been there to serve me. I was not in control, I felt... and I never would be.

Then one year I got a Raspberry Pi in my Christmas stocking. I learned how to flash an SD card with the RPi's version of linux. I learned how to tweak configuration files and install software. I was free to experiment with this little computer because it was cheap and unintimidating. It didn't contain my music, my scanned artwork, my photographs, my CV... if I somehow corrupted the operating system I could just reflash the SD card and start again.

I realised I didn't have to become an expert overnight. I could learn a little at a time, gaining confidence along the way. I learned how to take risks with my OS and precations with my data. I lost my fear of technology and started to take control of it.

It's hard for me to say how life-changing this has been for me, because I didn't just learn about computers, I learned something profound about life - that I could take control and change things. If I could overcome this hurdle, at the age of 35, what other problems could I overcome? If communities of programmers could self-organise to produce high-quality software that was free and open, maybe my youthful idealism wasn't as miguided as I'd grown to believe.

My whole worldview has been transformed by the change in my relationship with technology. I now have confidence to move into a career I can love and into which I channel my creative energies, my analytical skills and, hopefully, my sense of enthusiasm and political motivation as well. Front end web development seems like an ideal way to get started.
